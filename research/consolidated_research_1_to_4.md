# Portfolio & Trust Research Scratchpad (Batches 1-4)

## Batch 1: Endowment Models & Alternatives
- **Yale Endowment Model:** Focuses heavily on the illiquidity premium provided by alternative assets like Private Equity, Venture Capital, and Absolute Return (Hedge Funds), structurally diverging from standard 60/40 public portfolios. In 2024, targets aimed for ~5.7% returns but faced drag from private asset exit market illiquidity.
- **Family Office Shifts:** Global family offices are allocating 45% to 59% of their portfolios into alternatives in 2024, abandoning cash to lock in multi-generational wealth compounding regardless of the liquidity constraints.
- **Asset Breakdown:** Typical allocation trends heavily into Private Equity (~22-35%), Real Estate (~10-12%), and Private Debt (~4%).

## Batch 2: Tax Mechanics & Trust Structures
- **Texas Trust Code (HEMS):** Distributions governed by Health, Education, Maintenance, and Support provide an "ascertainable standard" that protects against creditors. Texas law views this broadly as maintaining the beneficiary's "accustomed standard of living," not just bare survival.
- **Distributable Net Income (DNI):** The maximum income passed through to beneficiaries to avoid double taxation. It typically excludes capital gains (which are taxed at the trust level unless specific provisions exist). Trust tax brackets are highly compressed, hitting the maximum 37% rate at extremely low income levels (~$15,900).
- **Grantor vs. Non-Grantor:** Grantor trusts pass the income tax burden entirely to the creator, allowing the trust assets to grow tax-free (an incredible estate reduction tool). Non-grantor trusts are separate entities, useful for SALT deduction maximization and QSBS stacking.
- **Asset Protection (WY vs SD vs NV):** Nevada offers no statutory exception creditors and a tiny 2-year statute of limitations. South Dakota abolished the rule against perpetuities for Dynasty Trusts. Wyoming allows 1,000-year terms with exceptional court privacy.

## Batch 3: Sequence of Returns & Withdrawal Modeling
- **Sequence of Returns Risk (SORR):** The math determining that poor market returns in the first 5 years of distributions can irreparably decimate a portfolio's longevity, regardless of long-term averages. Mitigated via 2-3 year cash buffers or dynamic rules.
- **Guyton-Klinger Rules:** Dynamic withdrawal logic. If the portfolio drops heavily (withdrawal rate spikes 20% above initial), you cut withdrawals by 10%. If the market booms, you increase withdrawals by 10%. Preserves capital mathematically.
- **Tax Efficient Vehicles (ETFs vs Mutual Funds):** High Net Worth logic dictates relying on ETFs over Mutual funds due to "in-kind" creation/redemption, which avoids triggering mandatory capital gains distributions to shareholders—a major flaw in standard mutual funds that generates unexpected tax bills.

## Batch 4: Private Markets Math & Historical Correlations
- **Box-Muller & Fat Tails:** Monte Carlo simulations using standard Box-Muller transforms underestimate "fat tail" extreme market events. Advanced modeling must use Student's t-distributions or jump-diffusion to accurately stress-test a $100k/day withdrawal scenario.
- **Sharpe vs. Sortino Ratios:** Private markets must be evaluated using the Sortino ratio. Sharpe penalizes all volatility (including massive upside gains), while Sortino only penalizes downside deviation, accurately reflecting the risk of illiquid capital preservation.
- **Private Credit Yield Premiums:** Direct lending provides a 1.5% to 2.4% (150-240 bps) premium over broadly syndicated loans. This "illiquidity premium" is protected by floating rates and stricter covenants, mitigating mark-to-market daily volatility.
- **Private Equity Drawdowns & J-Curve:** Historically, PE suffers -18% drawdowns during massive crises compared to -31% in public markets. The J-Curve mathematical model requires projecting initial negative cash flows (fees/calls) against back-loaded exits.
- **S&P 500 100-Year Baseline:** The inflation-adjusted real return of the S&P 500 over 100 years is a relentless 6.5% to 7.25% per year, establishing the ultimate benchmark. Real estate historically has a very low (sometimes 0.06) correlation to this, proving its diversification value.
- **FLP Valuation Discounts (IRS):** Family Limited Partnerships allow for Lack of Control (15-40%) and Lack of Marketability (10-30%) discounts, combining to drastically reduce taxable estate values. However, the IRS requires a strict non-tax "business purpose" to survive audit.
- **CRUT Tax Deferral:** Charitable Remainder Unitrusts bypass immediate capital gains tax upon the sale of highly appreciated assets. The trust sells the asset tax-free and distributes income in an IRS-tiered structure (Ordinary -> Capital Gains -> Tax-Exempt -> Principal return).
- **QSBS (Section 1202):** Provides up to a $10M or 10x basis federal capital gains exclusion for C-Corp stock acquired at issuance and held for 5 years, provided the company meets the $50M active asset test.
