Trust Intel Brief

Quick-reference tactical guide for meetings.
⚖️
1. Compliance & HEMS Standard
Your Angle

Trustees operate under total liability. They respond well to beneficiaries who understand "fiduciary bound" boundaries. The HEMS standard explicitly protects your accustomed standard of living under "Maintenance."

"My goal today is entirely educational. Before discussing strategy, I’d like to kindly request a copy of the trust deed, the IPS, and the latest accounting so I can understand the exact distribution parameters governing the trust."
If They Object (Response Matrix)
IF: "We don't normally share the full deed."
"Under Texas Trust Code, upon irrevocability, I have an absolute right as a primary beneficiary to the instrument and annual accountings."
⚙️
2. Portfolio Mathematics
Your Angle

Focus on the "Yale Smoothing Rule" W = 4% * [Avg(Yr1, Yr2, Yr3)]. This proves you understand long-term institutional capital preservation vs. dangerous flat-percentage draws.

"Regarding the asset allocation, what expected real return are you modeling for 2026? Are we pegging our sustainable withdrawal rate to a 3-year or 5-year trailing average endowment smoothing rule?"
If They Ask For Your Number
IF: "What kind of distributions are you needing?"
"My goal is roughly $100k daily distribution. I realize this requires roughly $912M in principal at a strictly managed 4% target. Where is the current corpus relative to that benchmark?"
🏛️
3. Trust Taxation (DNI & K-1s)
Your Angle

Trusts hit max federal tax (37%) at ONLY $15,200 of retained income. They *want* to distribute Distributable Net Income (DNI) to you, transferring the tax burden to your (hopefully lower) personal brackets. Texas has 0% state income tax.

"From a tax efficiency standpoint, how are we managing DNI to avoid compressed trust tax brackets? When distributions occur, what is the usual Schedule K-1 characterization breakdown between principal, capital gains, and ordinary income?"
📊
4. Institutional Risk Vectors
Your Angle

Separate the amateurs from the pros. Ask about "Tail Risk", "Max Drawdowns", and "Monte Carlo Simulation probabilities" over a 30-to-50 year horizon. Make them do the heavy math.

"Before we finalize the distribution model, I’d appreciate it if your team ran two scenarios for me. First, a Monte Carlo stress test showing the depletion risk over 30 years using a 4% smoothed draw rate on the current allocation. Second, what is the liquidity timeframe if we needed a sudden major un-modeled distribution?"
📝
5. The "Walk Out" Checklist
Tap to cross off in meeting
  • Get Original Trust Instrument (+Amendments)
  • Get Investment Policy Statement (IPS)
  • Get Latest Holdings / Accounting Statement
  • Get Full Direct & Indirect Fee Schedule
  • Ask for the Monte Carlo Depletion Report
"Thank you for your transparency today. Once I receive the documents and the Monte Carlo data we discussed, I’ll digest it and we can schedule a specific follow-up to solidify strategy."
