QUICK MATH (Shareable with managers)

Annual target = daily_target × 365

Portfolio expected return = Σ (weight_i × return_i)

Required capital = Annual target ÷ Portfolio expected return

Example: $100,000/day → $36,500,000/year.

If expected portfolio return = 6% → Required capital ≈ $36,500,000 ÷ 0.06 = $608,333,333

★ TAX NOTE (Texas Focus)

  • • Texas has no state income tax, but federal trust taxation still applies. Trustees reach top federal rates quickly; trusts pay 37% federal tax at relatively low retained-income levels.
  • • Distributable Net Income (DNI) is the trust calculation that determines what income shifts to beneficiaries (who then may report it on personal returns via Schedule K-1). That often reduces trust-level taxes but moves tax liability to beneficiaries.
  • Action item (ask now): "How are you allocating DNI between principal, capital gains, and ordinary income on K-1s? Show a sample K-1 for last distribution."

When is it 'Appropriate' to Take Profits?

  • • After liquidity events (asset sales) or when a reserve buffer covers 6–12 months of distributions.
  • • When the policy-approved spending rate (e.g., 3–5% smoothed) supports it under stress tests.
  • • After independent tax and estate counsel confirm tax efficiency.
  • • When trustees and beneficiaries approve in writing per trust rules.

Beginner Primer — trust types in one line
  • • Revocable trust: grantor controls assets; distributions flexible.
  • • Irrevocable trust: grantor gives up control; distributions only per document.
  • • Spendthrift trust: protects assets from beneficiary creditors.
  • • Totals: ask “which of these applies?” and have them point to the deed.
One-sentence IPS summary to ask for
  • • Request: “Please give me the Investment Policy Statement — I want the stated objective, risk tolerance, target return, and rebalancing rules in one paragraph.”
  • • Why: anchors expectations and performance comparisons.

Small glossary — HEMS, DNI, K-1 (plain English)
  • • HEMS = Health, Education, Maintenance, Support (common discretionary standard).
  • • DNI = Distributable Net Income (what can be taxed to beneficiaries on K-1).
  • • K-1 = tax schedule beneficiaries receive showing income passed through from trust.
Tax checklist to hand them (ask now)
  • • Are distributions taxable to the trust or beneficiary? (ask for sample K-1).
  • • How are capital gains allocated vs ordinary income?
  • • Do you use tax-loss harvesting? Show last 2 years of tax filings.
  • • Ask: “What estimated tax actions should a beneficiary take after receiving a distribution?”
Sample IPS clause — distribution policy COPY

“Spending Rule: The trust will distribute annually an amount equal to spending_rate × average(MV_t for past N years). The default spending_rate is 4% with N = 3 (smoothing). Floor: inflation-adjusted baseline; Ceiling: 6% unless committee approval.”

Liquidity sources (what they should show you)
  • • Cash & equivalents (overnight funds, MMFs) — immediate.
  • • Public securities — sell in 1–7 days depending on size.
  • • Private assets / RE / PE — 30–90+ days and may need valuation.
  • • Loans / credit facility — shows use of leverage; ask terms & covenants.

Quick guide: Is the trust likely to approve? (3 checks)
  • • Is there sufficient liquidity (cash buffer or sellable assets)? → Yes/No.
  • • Does the request fit the trust’s stated standard (mandatory vs discretionary)? → Yes/No.
  • • Does the scenario pass the stress test without undue depletion probability? → Yes/No.
  • • If two or more No’s → expect friction/delay or denial.
