1. The Fiduciary & Legal Anchor

Trustees in Texas operate under the "highest duty under the law". They are terrified of breaching this. Speak their language to lower their defenses.

HEMS Standard (IRS Safe Harbor)
Discretionary distributions are governed by HEMS: Health, Education, Maintenance, or Support. "Maintenance" legally protects your accustomed standard of living. Tie all requests to these pillars.
Duty of Prudent Management
Trustees must preserve the portfolio per the Texas Property Code. They will heavily scrutinize large, one-time cash drawdowns outside a policy.
Beneficiary Demand Rights
Under the Texas Trust Code, you possess the unalienable right to an annual accounting and a copy of the trust document upon irrevocability.
" Opening Posture (Tap to Read) 💬
"My goal today is educational. I'd like to ask for a copy of the trust deed, the IPS, and the latest accounting statements so I can understand the exact distribution standards—whether HEMS or otherwise—that govern this trust."

2. Sustaining the Endowment Model

How trusts guarantee perpetual asset survival using University Endowment methodologies.

Target Capital Requirement Capital Needed = Annual Target Payout ÷ Expected Portfolio Return

If you want $100k/day ($36.5M/yr), and the portfolio nets 6% annually:
$36.5M ÷ 0.06 = $608M required capital.
The Smoothing Rule (Yale Model) Safe Distribution = % Rate × (Average Portfolio Value over N years)

Endowments limit draws to 3-5% of a 3-to-5-year average value to avoid forced asset liquidation during market crashes.
" The Institutional Question ⚙️
"If we map this to an endowment model, what is the current sustainable withdrawal rate outlined in the IPS? Are we utilizing a 3-to-5-year smoothing rule to determine the safe distribution amount?"

3. Portfolio Stress Testing Request

Don't ask "how is it doing?" Ask for the hard math on catastrophic risk.

  • Max Drawdown: What is the worst peak-to-trough drop this combination of assets has experienced?
  • Monte Carlo Depletion Risk: Have they run a 10,000-trial simulation to prove the trust survives a 30-year span at the current payout rate?
  • Cash Buffer Allocation: Do they keep 6-12 months of distributions in liquid cash so they never sell stocks at a loss?
" The Scenario Request 📊
"Could your team run two scenarios for me? First, a Monte Carlo stress test showing the depletion risk at a 4% smoothed draw rate. Second, a liquidity analysis of what happens if we needed to raise a sudden cash distribution within 30 days."

4. Texas & Federal Tax Mechanics

Trust taxation is wildly different from personal taxation.

Distributable Net Income (DNI) Trap
Trusts hit the top 37% federal tax bracket at mere $15,200 of retained income. Trusts want to distribute this DNI to you, pushing the tax burden to your individual, lower tax brackets.
The K-1 Character Pass-Through
You receive a Schedule K-1. The "character" of the asset passes to you. Capital gains pass as capital gains (max 20%); interest passes as ordinary income.
The Texas Geographic Arbitrage
0% State Income Tax. You have a massive geometric advantage. You only plan for Federal IRS estimated payments on K-1 income.
" Tax Architecture Query 📝
"From a tax efficiency standpoint, how are we managing DNI to avoid compressed trust tax brackets? When distributions occur, what is the usual K-1 characterization breakdown between principal, capital gains, and ordinary income?"
